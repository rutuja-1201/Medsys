Overview

Building a brand takes a lot of effort and consistent hard work over time. So, it is very important to register your logo, word, sign, symbol, brand name, numerals or combination of colors, packaging labels, shape, sound, jingle, tagline or a combination used by a company to identify their own products and/or services in which you have undoubtedly invested a lot of blood, sweat and tears to enjoy the exclusive ownership of your creativity and create a distinct identity for your brand.

In India you can trademark any of the following items:

Device Brand Heading Label Tickets Name Signature Word Letter Numeral Shape Of Goods Packaging Jingle Tagline Combinaton Of Colors Any Such Combinations

The Controller General Of Patents, Designs and Trademarks, Ministry Of Industry and Commerce and Government Of India register trademarks in India. You can register the trademark under The Trademark Act, 1999.

Trademark Symbols

Trade Mark (TM) and Service Mark (SM)

This indicates that the trademark has not yet been registered, but that an application is in process. It is used to warn third parties that they have not violated it. This request has no particular legal significance as it has not yet been approved by the authorities.

R Symbol

As the trademark application is approved, you can see the R mark next to the trademark as the owner of the newly registered trademark. This indicates that the mark is officially registered and that any infringement by a third party is illegal.

    Process Flow

      Trademark Search (1 Day)

      TM search allows you to know whether there are similar trademarks out there and whether they are registered or not & thereby avoiding the chances of trademark litigation for you

      Filing of Trademark application (1 - 2 Days)

      An application for Trademark registration with the right class and correct details along with the requisite forms is to be submitted to the Office of the Controller General of Patents Designs & trademarks, who issues an acknowledgement number within one or two working days.

      Vienna Codification (3 - 4 Days)

      Once the application is filed, a Vienna code is assigned to the trademark by the Indian trademark registry if it contains some figurative elements/logo. This is done by the Registry to conduct trademark searches for artworks/logos

      Trademark Examination (12 - 18 Months)

      The trademark application is then reviewed by the Trademark Registry to confirm if the basic requirements are met, Based on this check, the registry prepares a trademark inspection report. It might take 12-18 months for examination.

      Journal Publication (4 Months)

      The specified trademarks will be published in the trademark journal after receipt of the trademark registration form the registrar. Once published, there is a chance that public objections will be made to public records that they believe may harm their business.

      Trademark Registration Certificate (1 Day)

      If there are no objections or contradictions to the request, a trademark manuscript and a trademark registration certificate will be prepared. Once it has been issued, the trademark will be considered the registered, allowing the owner to make exclusive use of the trademark.

      Renewal (After 10 Years)

      The validity of your registered trademark is for 10 years. After that, you are allowed to renew the registration for your trademark. Hence, you can perpetually protect your trademark
